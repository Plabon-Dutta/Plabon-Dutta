## About

I am Jayonto Dutta Plabon, a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Asia Pacific (UAP), Dhaka, Bangladesh, where I am also pursuing my Master of Science in Computer Science. My academic and professional journey is driven by a deep passion for research and a strong aspiration to pursue doctoral studies in the field of Artificial Intelligence.

My primary research interests span Large Language Models (LLMs), Generative AI, Federated Learning (FL), Natural Language Processing (NLP), Computer Vision, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and IoT. I have had the privilege of publishing research in high-impact, peer-reviewed venues including Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence, IEEE Access, and Computers and Electrical Engineering, as well as contributing book chapters to Elsevier publications — reflecting my commitment to advancing knowledge at the frontier of intelligent systems.

Alongside my academic responsibilities, I serve as Co-convenor of the UAP Programming Contest Club, where I mentor and guide students in competitive programming and algorithmic problem-solving. My own competitive programming background is notable — I am an ICPC Regionalist (2021, 2023, 2024) and a Specialist on Codeforces, which has sharpened my analytical thinking and problem-solving rigor that I bring into my research work.

Prior to my current role, I gained industry experience as an Implementation Engineer at DataSoft Systems Bangladesh Limited, working on scalable web applications using Java, Spring Boot, and Angular, and as a Research Intern at the Advanced Machine Intelligence Research Lab (AMIRL), where I contributed to deep learning research and academic publications.

I am currently seeking PhD opportunities where I can deepen my expertise in AI, contribute meaningfully to the global research community, and work alongside leading scholars to address real-world challenges through intelligent systems.
